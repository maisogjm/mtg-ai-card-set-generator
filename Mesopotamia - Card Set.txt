Mesopotamia
This Mesopotamia set is a three-color–friendly, historic-legendary themed environment built around legendary lands, creatures, and artifacts, with a strong subtheme of sagas, gods, and city-states. The general strategy is to develop powerful legendary mana bases like Ur, Uruk, Kish, Awan, Akkad, Babylon, Ashur, Nineveh, Twin Peaks of Mount Mashu, and Tigris and Euphrates, then leverage them with legendary payoffs such as Marduk, Enki, Enlil, Tiamat, the Primordial Sea, Gilgamesh, Enkidu, Inanna, Queen of Heaven, and many support pieces like The Lugal of Kish, The Sumerian King List, and Library of Ashurbanipal.

COLOR IDENTITY AND BASIC LANDS

Most of the set’s power lies in white, blue, black, red, and green, but the cards cluster most heavily in three colors: white, green, and black, with strong splashes for blue and red. To keep things manageable, a typical 60-card deck using this set as a core should aim for an Abzan (W/B/G) base with light U/R splashes enabled by the legendary lands and mana rocks.

For a 24-land deck built around these cards, a good starting mix of basic lands is:

6 Plains
5 Swamps
5 Forests
4 Islands
4 Mountains
Because many cards like Fertile Crescent, The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Marshland Irrigator, Clay Tablet Archive, Cuneiform Tablet, Ziggurat of Ur, Temple Astronomer of Ur, and Mesopotamian Frontier Scout help fix or ramp mana, you can rely less on exact basic ratios in practice, but the above mix supports casting the bulk of spells while still enabling blue and red legends.

SYNERGY: LEGENDARY LANDS AND LEGENDARY PAYOFFS

The core synergy is between the legendary city lands—Ur, Uruk, Kish, Awan, Akkad, Babylon, Ashur, Nineveh, Twin Peaks of Mount Mashu, and Tigris and Euphrates—and legendary payoffs.

• Ur fixes white/blue and can scry and gain life, fueling control strategies alongside Waters of Death and House of Dust. • Uruk fixes red/green and grants trample and power boosts, perfect for Gugalanna, the Bull of Heaven, Gilgamesh, Enkidu, Humbaba, and Desert Caravan Trader–fueled attacks. • Kish fixes white/black and can produce any color specifically for legendary spells, enabling multicolor legends like Marduk or Inanna, Queen of Heaven. • Awan fixes green/blue and grows new creatures, synergizing with Gilgamesh’s counters and The Sumerian King List’s token strategies. • Akkad fixes red/black and grants menace, fitting aggressive decks with Guti, Amorites, Assyrian War Chariot, and Sennacherib. • Babylon supports recursion of small creatures like Mesopotamian Frontier Scout, Clay Brick Mason, River Reed Scribe, and Underworld Ferryman, all of which feed the historic engine. • Ashur produces red/white and creates Warrior tokens if you control a legendary creature; these pair well with The Lugal of Kish and Assyrian War Chariot. • Nineveh provides blue/black and mills, fueling House of Dust, The Annuna, Judges of the Underworld, and Underworld Ferryman. • Twin Peaks of Mount Mashu adds red/green and exiles cards to play, especially strong with many high-impact legends like Marduk or Tiamat, the Primordial Sea. • Tigris and Euphrates ramps into green/blue and animates lands; it also draws cards when legendary lands become creatures, combining nicely with Fertile Crescent and The Hanging Gardens of Babylon by turning your mana base into threats.

These lands are enhanced by Fertile Crescent, which places growth counters on lands and turns them into rainbow sources, and by The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, which creates Plants that can be sacrificed for any color to cast legendary spells. Marshland Irrigator, Mesopotamian Frontier Scout, Clay Tablet Archive, Cuneiform Tablet, Ziggurat of Ur, and Temple Astronomer of Ur all further stabilize your colors and reward playing many lands or legendary cards.

SYNERGY: LEGENDARY MATTERS AND HISTORIC PAYOFFS

Many cards explicitly reward you for controlling or casting historic spells (legendaries, sagas, and artifacts).

• Marduk triggers whenever you cast a historic spell (including artifacts like Cuneiform Tablet, Library of Ashurbanipal, Ziggurat of Ur, Tablets of Destiny, The Sumerian King List, Ark of Utnapishtim, Assyrian War Chariot, and Clay Tablet Archive, or sagas like Epic of Gilgamesh). Each trigger lets you either deal 2 damage to any target or loot, making every historic play a tempo or card advantage boost. • Enki untaps lands and other permanents at end step and gives you life or a bounce whenever you cast a legendary spell, dovetailing with heavy-legend builds. • Enlil gives all legendary creatures a mana ability to cast more legendary spells, and his attack taps down permanents, especially punishing once you have three or more legendary permanents. • Inanna, Queen of Heaven creates Clerics whenever you cast a historic spell; sacrificing them reanimates legendary creatures like Gilgamesh, Enkidu, Gugalanna, Marduk, or Enki. • Temple Astronomer of Ur scries whenever a land enters, and if that land is legendary you scry 2 and can draw a legendary card, strongly tying legendary lands to card selection. • Library of Ashurbanipal scries on each historic cast and can turn discarded legendary cards into extra draws, synergizing nicely with River Reed Scribe and Nebuchadnezzar the Great. • Cuneiform Tablet and Clay Tablet Archive both produce mana for historic or legendary spells, and Cuneiform Tablet can cheat a legendary card into play tapped. • Ziggurat of Ur gives flexible colored mana for creatures and untaps when you cast legendary creature spells, effectively doubling as a ritual for legends. • Tablets of Destiny lets you play legendary lands from the top of your library and occasionally cast a legendary card for free, making heavy-legend builds extremely explosive.

The Lugal of Kish is a central enchantment: it makes you the monarch whenever a legendary creature enters and then gives legendary creatures +1/+1 and ward if you remain monarch. This pairs beautifully with token makers like The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Ashur, Assyrian War Chariot, and The Sumerian King List, and with resilient legends like Enkidu, Inanna, Queen of Heaven, and Underworld Ferryman.

The Sumerian King List chooses a creature type—often Human, Warrior, or Lamassu—and buffs legendary creatures of that type, while making Citizen tokens of the chosen type. Those tokens can be turned into meaningful threats with Gilgamesh’s counter-moving, Gugalanna’s damage, or Assyrian War Chariot’s attack bonuses.

SYNERGY: GILGAMESH NARRATIVE PACKAGE

Several cards specifically interlock around the Gilgamesh myth.

• Gilgamesh enters with +1/+1 counters equal to the number of other legendary permanents you control, making him huge in a developed board. He can move counters from other permanents onto himself when he attacks, stealing counters from Enkidu, Humbaba, Plants from The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, or lands animated by Tigris and Euphrates. • Enkidu gets extra counters if you control Uruk, and he gains indestructible if you control Gilgamesh. When Enkidu dies, he transfers two counters to another creature, often back to Gilgamesh or a god like Marduk or Enlil. • Humbaba forces opponents to sacrifice creatures with flying or reach and repeatedly fights opposing creatures whenever you play lands, especially useful with land-ramp from Fertile Crescent, Mesopotamian Frontier Scout, and Marshland Irrigator. • Epic of Gilgamesh tutors Gilgamesh or Enkidu, then creates a Beast token representing Humbaba’s Guardian, and finally grants a massive overrun-like effect to legendary creatures with trample and a death-draw trigger. This final chapter combines with Gilgamesh’s on-death card draw, Gugalanna’s trample, and Marduk’s damage to finish games. • Two Great Stone Monsters can sacrifice itself to protect a legendary creature from targeted effects, keeping Gilgamesh or Enkidu alive at key moments. • Waters of Death exiles attackers and grants scry and life if you control legendary creatures or lands, reflecting the peril of Gilgamesh’s journey.

Together, Gilgamesh, Enkidu, Humbaba, Epic of Gilgamesh, Two Great Stone Monsters, Waters of Death, Twin Peaks of Mount Mashu, and Ark of Utnapishtim form a self-contained narrative engine that is also strategically powerful.

SYNERGY: GODS, UNDERWORLD, AND JUDGMENT

The divine and underworld cards create a recursive, grindy gameplan.

• Tiamat, the Primordial Sea creates Serpents equal to your legendary count and can sacrifice legendary permanents to counter spells that target you or your permanents. This pairs with token-producing legendaries like The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Ashur, Inanna, Queen of Heaven, and The Sumerian King List. • House of Dust shrinks all creatures and mills whenever they die, filling graveyards for Underworld Ferryman, Babylon, Inanna, Queen of Heaven, and The Annuna, Judges of the Underworld. • The Annuna, Judges of the Underworld exiles graveyard creatures and drains life or grows your legends when any creature card is exiled. This is particularly potent with Nineveh’s milling and exiling, Waters of Death’s exile, and Great Flood’s sweeping destruction. • The Two Galla tutors for Inanna, Queen of Heaven or House of Dust, then drains life each time a legendary creature enters, synergizing with token recursion and multi-legend turns enabled by Tablets of Destiny. • Underworld Ferryman reanimates small creatures like Mesopotamian Frontier Scout, Clay Brick Mason, River Reed Scribe, and Bronze-Helmed Spearman, and comes back when legends die, supporting attrition strategies. • Great Flood is a powerful sweeper that spares legendary permanents and replaces destroyed legendaries with basic lands, synergizing with a deck full of legends and ramp payoffs like Fertile Crescent and Temple Astronomer of Ur.

SYNERGY: WAR AND EMPIRE

The imperial cards reward aggressive, combat-focused play.

• Sargon grows whenever legendary lands enter and buffs other attacking legendary creatures, pushing a go-wide legendary attack. • Sennacherib taps and locks down opposing lands when your creatures connect and shrinks or kills creatures whenever your legendary lands tap, combining with menace from Akkad and Guti, and evasion from Winged Lion. • Nebuchadnezzar the Great offers versatile ETB/attack modes and turns all your legendary lands into rainbow sources for historic spells, solving multicolor issues and enabling big plays with Marduk, Tiamat, the Primordial Sea, and Great Flood. • Guti pressures hands with discard and grows via Raid, pairing well with Battle of Carchemish’s debuff and menace from Akkad and Desert Caravan Trader. • Kassites fetches any land (including legendary cities) and gains life when lands enter, or grows when they’re legendary, making it a key fixer. • Amorites grants haste and power boosts when you control a legendary land and can be sacrificed for multicolored mana for legends. • Assyrian War Chariot grants haste and boosts attacking legendary creatures, combining strongly with Sargon, Aqrabuamelu, The Scorpion-Men, and Enlil. • Battle of Carchemish doubles as a mass debuff or a draw engine on legendary combat damage, and can be cast at instant speed if you have three or more legendaries. • Aqrabuamelu, The Scorpion-Men locks down nonlegendary creatures and gives all your legendary creatures vigilance, perfect for attacking while still holding up blockers and mana abilities from Enlil.

SYNERGY: CITY DEFENSE, LAMASSU, AND ARTISANS

The city-defense and artisan cards support both stall and ramp strategies.

• Winged Bull gains ward while you control a legendary land and boosts other Lamassu’s toughness, making it a key defensive piece. • Winged Lion taps blockers on attack and gains vigilance if you control another Lamassu, pairing naturally with Winged Bull. • Bronze-Helmed Spearman and City Gate Watch both become more formidable when you control legendary lands, representing fortified cities. • Clay Brick Mason ramps into artifacts and enchantments, growing when you cast legendary ones like The Lugal of Kish, Code of Hammurabi, The Curse of Agade, Library of Ashurbanipal, Ziggurat of Ur, Tablets of Destiny, The Sumerian King List, Ark of Utnapishtim, and Epic of Gilgamesh. • River Reed Scribe loots on ETB and can be recurred when you discard legendary cards, synergistic with Library of Ashurbanipal, Nebuchadnezzar the Great, and Tablets of Destiny. • Desert Caravan Trader generates Treasures and converts sacrificed artifacts into menace and power boosts, fitting into aggressive or midrange shells built around Cuneiform Tablet, Clay Tablet Archive, and Assyrian War Chariot.

CURSES, LAWS, AND CONTROL

Control and midrange decks can leverage the legalistic and curse-themed cards.

• Code of Hammurabi punishes nonlegendary creatures that hit you and turns legendary deaths into card draw, encouraging you to rely heavily on legendary creatures and tokens. • The Curse of Agade forces an immediate legendary sacrifice and then punishes opponents who accumulate legendary permanents, ideal against other historic-heavy decks. • House of Dust plus Nineveh and The Annuna, Judges of the Underworld form a soft lock on creature strategies by shrinking, milling, and exiling creatures. • Waters of Death, Great Flood, and Battle of Carchemish provide layers of removal and board reset.

GENERAL STRATEGY USING THIS SET

A strong Mesopotamia deck typically:

Establishes a reliable mana base by playing a mix of basics (Plains, Swamps, Forests, Islands, Mountains) and legendary lands like Ur, Uruk, Kish, Awan, Akkad, Babylon, Ashur, Nineveh, Twin Peaks of Mount Mashu, and Tigris and Euphrates, supported by Fertile Crescent, The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Marshland Irrigator, Mesopotamian Frontier Scout, Clay Tablet Archive, Cuneiform Tablet, and Ziggurat of Ur.
Deploys early historic enablers such as Clay Brick Mason, Temple Astronomer of Ur, River Reed Scribe, Mesopotamian Frontier Scout, and Desert Caravan Trader to smooth draws and fix mana.
Drops midgame legends—Gilgamesh, Enkidu, Marduk, Enki, Enlil, Inanna, Queen of Heaven, Tiamat, the Primordial Sea, Sargon, Sennacherib, Nebuchadnezzar the Great, Guti, Kassites, Amorites, Aqrabuamelu, The Scorpion-Men—to seize board control and generate ongoing value from historic casts and legendary lands.
Uses synergies like The Lugal of Kish, The Sumerian King List, The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Library of Ashurbanipal, Tablets of Destiny, and Epic of Gilgamesh to snowball advantage, while relying on removal and control tools like Waters of Death, House of Dust, The Annuna, Judges of the Underworld, Battle of Carchemish, The Curse of Agade, Code of Hammurabi, and Great Flood to manage opposing threats.
Closes the game with overwhelming legendary boards (often after Epic of Gilgamesh’s third chapter), trampling attackers like Gugalanna, the Bull of Heaven, Gilgamesh, and Humbaba, evasive gods like Marduk and Enlil, or repeated value and recursion from Inanna, Queen of Heaven, Underworld Ferryman, Ark of Utnapishtim, and Tiamat, the Primordial Sea.
All 48 cards—from the city-lands like Ur and Babylon to mythic figures like Marduk and Inanna, support creatures like Marshland Irrigator and Bronze-Helmed Spearman, and artifacts like Ark of Utnapishtim and Assyrian War Chariot—are designed to interlock around the historic and legendary themes, making a cohesive and synergistic Mesopotamian experience.
